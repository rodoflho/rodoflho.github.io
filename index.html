The look of Bacchus

Representation of the famous work, 'The Triumph of Bacchus,' by Diego Vélasquez.

This painting juxtaposes mythology with everyday life. The canvas represents the god Bacchus as the god offering men the wine that frees them temporarily from their problems. In Baroque literature, Bacchus is considered as an allegory for the liberation of man from the slavery of daily life.

2017 - MAY 2019

Hello, my name is Rebecca Timoteo, a passionate 25 years old Brazilian artist with a deep love for marble. I am constantly inspired by its beauty and mystery.

My journey as an artist began many years ago in the Brazilian countryside, where I was raised in a family that holds values of love. My strongest inspiration came from my mother, whose words and wisdom invited me into new worlds of thought and feeling. She once said, "art is a portrait of a time," and that statement sparked my curiosity about the people and emotions that surrounded me. It also instilled in me a deep desire to express myself through art.

Since then, I have honed my skills and techniques, learning from the great artists who came before me and finding my own voice in the process. One of my dreams as an artist is to create works of marble that will inspire and delight audiences around the world. To achieve this dream, I know that I will need the guidance and support of an experienced mentor - someone who can help me develop my skills and techniques and guide me on my journey as an artist. I am always eager to learn and grow as an artist, and I believe that we learn so much and grow by sharing your knowledge for other people.

Virtual Reality esposition

The photographic project 'Flou Révélateur' that I present in the form of a virtual reality exhibition proposal staged a conception of blur. Through portraits, people i meet, I tell you about my daily life. These images appeal to each other, they create tension. The contrast between the blur of people crossing and the sharpness of our own image. The choice of the virtual reality exhibition fits into the times we are living today. I wish, through this format, to show that we can continue to exhibit works, encourage creations and offer new models of expression to artists.
